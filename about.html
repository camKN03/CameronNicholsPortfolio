About me.

I'm Cameron K. Nichols, a recent graduate of Dickinson College with a B.A. in Middle East Studies and an Arabic minor, and an incoming M.A. student in Global Governance, Politics, and Security at American University's School of International Service. I'm based in the Washington, D.C. area.

Right now I work as an Energy Infrastructure Associate at Tangelic, a climate tech NGO focused on West African energy grid development. I co-produce the Tangelic Talks podcast, write analytical memos, and support research that connects energy policy with on-the-ground infrastructure needs.

Before that, I interned with MECGA in Doha, Qatar, where I conducted OSINT and conflict research while living through active regional escalation firsthand. That experience shaped how I think about security, information, and the distance between policy analysis and real-world stakes.
